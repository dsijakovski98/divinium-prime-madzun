ABOUT US

The story of our MADZUN begins in the distant 1896 when grandmother Maria decided to make madzun to use it as a sweetener for her delicious cakes and sweet bread. Over the years she perfected the recipe which became family inheritance. Nowadays the decision to register as a company and to place out madzun on the Macedonian market was based on our wish to present our traditional product to a wide audience. Through our madzun we not only offer you premium quality but also family tradition.

The history of the MADZUN

Grape molasses also known as MADZUN, is a 100% natural concentrate of dehydrated grape must from white or red grapes varieties. The madzun and its medicinal and nutritional properties were known long ago. The Madzun as a drink, medicine and as a 100% substitute for sugar and other sweeteners have been known to our ancestors for over two thousand years and the story of the madzun is as old as the story about the wine.In recent History in Macedonia specificaly In the Tikvesh wine region the MADZUN has always been used as a substitute for sugar because it was in abundance and the sugar itself was too expensive at the time and people could not afford it and went for the cheaper variant of sweetening with madzun, unaware that using this divine food daily, had a beneficial effect on their health.

The making process

Тhe production of the madzun starts with the beginning of the grape harvest in the Tikvesh wine region. To make the most delicious madzun we select the best and the sweetest grape varieties. Harvesting is the first step in madzun making process. The grapes are harvested in plastic crates to avoid damage during transportation and to prevent fermentation. After the grapes are sorted, they are ready to be de-stemmed and crushed. For many years, men and women did this manually by stomping the grapes with their feet. Nowadays, we perform this mechanically.

Mechanical grape crushers stomp or trod the grapes into what is called must. Must is simply freshly pressed grape juice that contains the skins, seeds, and solids. Pressing comes immediately after crushing. Using a grape press we separate the juice from the skins and seeds. The grape juice is then left overnight to sediment. After the sediment is sank to the bottom the clarified juice is ready for the next process. This process involves treatment of the juice with low and controlled heat which is under 80°C ,to ensure that all the nutrients stay intact, until almost all the water has evaporated and we are left with viscous and syrupy product with high natural sugar content.

The benefits

For the madzun it is said to be as nutritious as mother's milk. It is rich in minerals (potassium, sodium, calcium, phosphorus, iron), vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B6, C, A, D), energy substances, tannins, flavonoids, antioxidants, grape sugar, organic acids. That's why, the madzun is a real treasure trove of nutrients that will help improve one's health.

It is recommended for the strengthening of the immune system in children, convalescents, elderly people and athletes. It helps with colds and infections of the respiratory system, psychological disorders, overweight, constipation, anemia, diseases of the cardiovascular system, and is especially recommended for people with impaired liver function due to overactivity of jaundice, alcoholism, cirrhosis and excessive drug use. Due to its antioxidant properties it also acts anticancerogenic.

The madzun is made from only one ingredient which is grape and it is a 100% natural product with no added sugar, additives and preservatives.
